a genuine business tradeoff the program faced.

## Why I wrote this

I wanted to practice the kind of thinking a transformation advisor actually does:
reading a real, messy program (not a clean textbook example), reconstructing how its
success was actually measured, and forming a defensible point of view on a hard
tradeoff, using standard consulting case methodology (problem statement, options
considered, recommendation, risks).

## What's inside

- **Situation and background** on Clorox's five-year, 21-plant S/4HANA transformation
- **A reconstructed value realization scorecard**, built from the real operational and
  adoption metrics Clorox and Accenture tracked after go-live
- **A real tradeoff analysis**: Clorox's ERP stabilization overlapped with its
  announced acquisition of GOJO Industries. I lay out the options they had, the call
  they made, and my own recommendation, including what the public record leaves
  unanswered
- **Named risks and open questions**, including a technical question (clean core vs.
  custom code) that the public record doesn't answer, but that materially changes the
  risk profile of the decision

## A note on what this is

I have no connection to Clorox, Accenture, or SAP. This is my own independent analysis
based entirely on public reporting (sources linked and cited in the full document).
Anywhere the public record doesn't give a specific number or rationale, the analysis
says so directly instead of filling the gap.

## Format

Written in standard consulting case format: Executive Summary, Situation and
Background, Problem Statement, Analysis, Options Considered, Recommendation, Risks and
Open Questions.
